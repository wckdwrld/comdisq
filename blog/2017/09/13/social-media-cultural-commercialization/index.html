Social Media: Cultural Commercialization

The soul of this project is very much born from the idea of repossesion of the digital-social in an attempt to reconnect people in a meaningful and constructive manor. This repossession is crucial to the preservation of cultural authenticity, but to understand why we need to look into the alienation and exploitation of social media prosummers.

Marx considered alienation to be the disruption of the relationship between worker and their produce. In a industrial world, this manifest in the breakdown of the production process, whereby workers roles were specialized to the point of complete disconnection from the final product. A worker is responcible for one tiny part of thousands of units, as opposed to being responcible for few complete units; abstracting their involvement in the process. Now, consider the process of social media interaction, whereby users are coerced into regular production of information in attempt to fill a superficial social obligation to update the internet with their lives. Through participating in this process the prosumer alienates themselves from their product, information. This social information is foundational for culture.
